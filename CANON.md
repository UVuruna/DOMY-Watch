## The Web of Correspondences

The foundation everything hangs on is ONE web binding, per seat:

**color ↔ virtue ↔ vice ↔ emotion ↔ human trait ↔ mood ↔ state**

A seat (a weekday, an arm, a slot position) is never just a color —
it is the whole BUNDLE. Tuesday is not "orange": it is
orange–Courage–Wrath–Zeal–the Soldier, all at once. The authoritative
bundles live in [DOMY Symbolism](SYMBOLISM.md): the six arm colors
and their moods ("Colors as Moods of the Day"), the virtue/vice pairs
and their cross-cure axes, the professions, the theological trio.

Every current and future theme must find, for each of its members,
the seat whose bundle matches that member best. A figure is seated by
its NATURE against the bundle — never by name etymology or surface
resemblance alone (the Montu lesson: "Montu is literally Mars" seats
a war-god nobody knows over bundles that fit better — fame and
nature outrank a literal match).

<a id="seating-law"></a>

## The Seating Law

When composing a theme's membership, two approaches exist and BOTH
are sought in every pick, as far as possible:

- **(a) The Planetary fit** — the best, most ideal match to the
  seat's bundle AND to what the day currently means in the English
  week (Sun-day, Moon-day, … the day-ruler canon). This is the
  default roster.
- **(b) The Pantheon fit** — the most REPRESENTATIVE persons,
  figures, objects or phenomena of the theme itself, seated by their
  real rank and role in their own hierarchy on OUR archetypes. The
  supreme figure takes the throne (the Sun seat).

The ideal member scores on both. When they diverge, the two rosters
exist precisely to hold both answers (see below).

Supporting rules, all owner-decided:

- **Fame first.** Never keep an unknown figure over a famous one —
  we FORGE the symbolism to justify the seat; the argument follows
  the pick, not the other way around.
- **Max coverage.** The Pantheon roster prefers figures the
  Planetary roster does not already seat (Artemis over Ares on
  Tuesday, Hera on Friday, Frigg, Bastet) — together the two rosters
  should show as much of the culture as possible.
- **Narrative-first remap.** A member goes to the day its OWN canon
  points at (each religion sits on the day its scripture/practice
  names — never merely its rest day).
- **No seat left unarguable.** Every seat gets a full re-argued
  article for its position; reusing another seat's text is allowed
  only when the figure sits on the SAME seat in both rosters.

<a id="two-rosters"></a>

## The Two Rosters

Themes with a pantheon table carry two casts, picked PER SLOT in the
theme's own dropdown (like the metals):

- **Planetary** (default) — day-ruler counterparts, approach (a).
- **Pantheon** — the culture's own hierarchy, approach (b).

The safety law binds both: a figure's identity (name + article) only
ever appears together with its plate on disk — a pantheon seat whose
art has not landed keeps the WHOLE planetary bundle (file + name +
article), and a missing pantheon dual pulls the whole Sunday pair
back together. Implemented in `config.defaults.pantheon_seat`.

<a id="throne-shadow"></a>

## The Throne and Its Shadow

The Sunday center is a DUAL: the bright face and the shadow face of
the same throne — ruler and servant, day and underworld. Opposition
is NOT evil: Hades governs, he does not tempt. Current duals:
Zeus/Hades, Odin/the Wanderer, Perun/Veles, Ra's pair,
Christianity/Satanism (Creeds). Every changed sun seat needs its own
dual resolved — the dual follows the seated sun figure.

<a id="the-ninth"></a>

## The Ninth — Outside the Circle

Every theme may name a NINTH: a figure or phenomenon outside the
eight seats, of two kinds — the **Excluded** (one who belongs to the
story but not the circle) or the **Union** (the two extremes held
together). The ninth need not be a person (a place, an event, a
concept qualify) and is NEVER less important than the seated eight.
Locked ninths: Gaia, Yggdrasil, Triglav, The Pharaoh, The Polymath,
Freemasonry, The Unknown God (Ancient religions), The Holy Trinity,
Melchizedek (Bible II), The Ninth Circle (Dante). Sigma is the union
of Alpha and Omega. The scale's stained-glass register carries TWO
unions: **The Meeting** (figurative — the imaginary meeting where
each extreme tempers the other's madness; objectivity as PERSONS)
and **The Seal** (symbolic — the rosette whose overlap is clear
glass; objectivity as the only glass that does not color the light).

## Pointer Archetypes

Owner canon 2026-07-16: each pointer carries its own ARCHETYPE
reading of the arm colors — persons layered on the same wheel.
Approved so far (Seasons and Compass pending — the owner decides;
do not invent them):

### Trinity (trio)

The triangle: two natures below, the one who holds BOTH rises to
the apex.

| Arm | Color | Person | Nature | Type | Calling |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| top (12h) | yellow | **God** | both natures at once — loves himself AND questions himself, so he corrects his own deeds and cannot be manipulated or steered | the whole | **Judge** |
| 20h | red | **the Devil** | Pride — love turned toward himself | the Boss syndrome (everyone must please him) | **Prosecutor** |
| 04h | blue | **Jesus** | Weakness — love turned toward others | the Servant syndrome (pleases everyone) | **Advocate** |

Trinity archetype articles run in TWO ROWS (like the hexa zodiac
diamonds): the person and the calling.

### Prism (hexa)

Six arms, two interlocked triangles, and ONE mechanic: love can turn
flaws into virtues, and hatred can turn the same virtues into flaws.

| Arm | Color | Quality |
|---|---|---|
| top (12h) | yellow | **Love** — shared summit of Weakness and Pride: love of others AND love of self, at the point where they become SIN |
| 16h | orange | **Courage** |
| 20h | red | **Pride** |
| bottom (24h) | purple | **Hatred** — shared root of the virtue pair: turned inward it drags Humility down (Judas); turned outward it turns Courage into wrath, madness, overreach (Lucifer) |
| 04h | blue | **Weakness (Fear)** |
| 08h | green | **Humility** |

The diagonals carry the oppositions — **Love–Hatred** (12h–24h),
**Humility–Pride** (08h–20h), **Fear–Courage** (04h–16h) — the same
diagonal geometry as the mood and virtue axes in
[DOMY Symbolism](SYMBOLISM.md) (Fear–Courage IS the Moon–Mars
cross-cure axis).

Display rules (feature spec in [Roadmap](ROADMAP.md)): archetypes
show only during GLOW windows (a moon phase ~every 7 days, a sun
event ~every 90); the moon window reveals them only on Omega
double-click; the sun window also as a slot theme option and in the
Encyclopedia; they work under Time Travel.

<a id="working-rules"></a>

## Working Rules for Agents

- **One theme = one prompt file** (`research/prompts/`) — every
  variant of a theme lives in that theme's file; zero exceptions.
- **Owner assets are authoritative** — never code around an asset
  quirk; report the discrepancy and let the owner choose.
- **Accuracy > speed** — this is an astronomical instrument; pin
  every fixed behavior with a golden test before relying on it.
- **The safety law** (identity travels with its plate) applies to
  every new resolver — classic unit, seated slots, hovers alike.
- **When the owner's spec and an existing text disagree, ask** — the
  specs in `prompt.txt`/`INSTRUCTION.txt` are product decisions;
  fold them into docs and config, and leave the owner's scratchpad
  files untouched.
