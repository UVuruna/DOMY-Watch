# Symbolism Consistency Audit — Merged Report

## Executive Summary

Structural inventory is clean: all 11 weekday themes have complete 7/7 art matching `WEEKDAY_THEME_FILES` exactly, all three METAL_THEMES (greek, norse, profession) have complete `colored/` subfolders, and all ten article sets in `symbolism.json` carry the Sunday-duality paragraph with no exceptions. Three auditor passes were commissioned for this sweep — consistency, mythology accuracy, and moral-axes logic — and all three returned actionable findings, merged below into one report.

The consistency pass's findings cluster around one root cause: the 2026-07-12 owner decision swapping `religion_alt`'s Sunday god from Ancient Egypt to Mithraism reached the live article content, `config/defaults.py`, and `SYMBOLISM.md`, but two `symbolism.json` summary fields and one test docstring still describe the old Egypt-on-Sunday state (2 swap-recommended items). A second, unrelated gap: the fully-built Japan theme (art + articles + menu title) has no canon paragraph in `SYMBOLISM.md`, whose own article-set count is stale by exactly that missing theme (9×7=63 should be 10×7=70) — alongside a tick-dial arithmetic claim that does not sum to 360 and a vestigial per-body `"name"` json field the app never reads (4 improve items). Two lowest-severity items are noted for awareness only — a stale test comment and a dead legacy `center`/`axes` json block never migrated to the double-center canon.

The mythology-accuracy pass cross-checked all 63 non-trivial entity assignments across the 9 fully-elaborated themes (greek, norse, egypt, slavic, alchemy, japan, religion, religion_alt, profession) against independent scholarship — interpretatio graeca/germanica, planetary-week etymology (Tiw/Woden/Thor/Frigg-Freyja), the East Asian Wu Xing day-star tradition, Slavic reconstruction scholarship (Ivanov-Toporov), Egyptian astral theology, and Mithraic/Babylonian sources — and found the canon unusually well researched, with no spelling errors in any native-script display name (Greek polytonic, Old Norse diacritics, kanji/Hepburn, Slavic carons all check out). One genuine categorical tension surfaced: Freemasonry occupies the `religion` set's Tuesday/Mars slot despite not being classified as a religion by mainstream scholarship or Freemasonry's own governing bodies (1 improve item, with a concrete Zoroastrianism swap proposal). Three other borderline-looking picks — Slavic Mokoš for Venus, Norse Loki for Saturn, Islam for Venus in the base religion set — were checked against the "obvious" stronger-sounding alternative and in each case the current pick held up better (3 fine-but-noted confirmations, no swaps).

The moral-axes pass verified the underlying hour math directly and confirmed the color-mood "opposite" claims and the virtue cross-cure axes sit on the exact same three diametric arm-pairs — the emotional-day geometry and the moral cross-cure geometry are one consistent structure, the system's strongest feature, needing no change. The seven professions and the octa dawn/dusk pair were also checked and found sound. Two concrete weak points surfaced: the Saturn mythic anchor cites a Cronus myth (fear of a prophecy) that doesn't match Saturn's actual Patience/Envy pairing in this canon — Fear belongs to the Moon — making it a dangling, mismatched citation (1 swap-recommended item); and the purple/midnight color pairing is the one arm color with no atmospheric justification, unlike blue/pre-dawn, red/evening and green/morning, resting on heraldic/cultural symbolism alone (1 improve item). A documentation-clarity nit in the Theological Trio's two different Hope-hour placements, plus a requested reference paragraph on the physics of sky color for the Encyclopedia, round out that pass's fine-but-noted items (2 items).

Combined across all three passes: **3 swap-recommended, 6 improve, 7 fine-but-noted** — all listed below; the owner decides.

| Encyclopedia — scientific basis for blue-morning/red-evening sky color (requested reference) | No scientific explanation of sky color currently on file for the Encyclopedia article. | N/A — this is a supplied reference paragraph for potential use, not a defect. | Sky and sunlight color across the day is governed by Rayleigh scattering: air molecules scatter short (blue) wavelengths far more strongly than long (red) wavelengths. At midday the sun is high, so its light takes the shortest possible path through the atmosphere — little blue is scattered out of the direct beam (it looks close to white, color temperature roughly 5,500–6,000 K) while the scattered skylight all around reads pure blue.
