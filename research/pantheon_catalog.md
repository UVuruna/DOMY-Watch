**Purpose (owner 2026-07-15):** the review document — every theme,
every seat, BOTH modes side by side: **Planetary** (the shipped

## Doctrine crystallization (owner round two, 2026-07-15)

**1. ✅ APPROVED — the Underworld lives at the DUAL's shadow face.**
The owner's ruling: *"prava suprotnost — vrhovni bog i bog podzemlja,
koja god mitologija priča o tome."* The Sunday dual was never
strictly Ruler/Servant — that is the professions/planets theology
only. Our shipped dual pairs read Helios/**Phaethon** (the fallen
son), Sól/**Skoll** (the devourer), Sun/**Black Hole** (the collapse
pole), Gold/**Raw Ore**, Lucifer/**Judas**. The dual is THE BRIGHT
FACE AND ITS SHADOW, theme by theme — and the opposition NEED NOT be
evil (the owner's Bible reading: the Father and the Son oppose as
proud opposes humble, not as good opposes evil). Therefore: **the
Pantheon Greek dual is Zeus / Hades** — the throne above and the
throne below — and every mythology that tells the
throne-above/throne-below story may use the same pattern. Hades
stands on the dial at 24h all week and OPAQUE every Sunday.

**2. ✅ APPROVED with the owner's refinement — the Ninth, final
form.** Outside the week's circle, in one of two forms: the
**Excluded One**, or the **UNION of the week's poles** — and the
Union **need not be a person**: it can be a place, an event, a
concept. The owner's own examples define it: no mythology offers one
person who is both Lucifer and Judas — but **HELL** holds them both,
at the very bottom, per Dante; and in the Bible, where the Father is
the Sunday face and the Son its dual, the ninth is **the Holy
Trinity** — the union of the pair itself. Two laws follow:
- **The Union must NEVER rank below the seated figures** — it is the
  hardest entry to define in every theme, and worth the effort.
- Each theme is decided ON ITS OWN (the per-theme review below);
  where no worthy Union exists, the Excluded form stands proudly.
Sigma's article reframes now: the union of Alpha and Omega in one
body, possibly stronger than Alpha — never a lesser exile.

**3. ✅ APPROVED — the max-coverage principle.** Where the symbolism
fits CLEANLY, the Pantheon roster prefers a figure the Planetary
roster does NOT already seat — the two rosters together should cover
as many first-rank figures as possible. A repeat is allowed only
where no fresh figure carries the seat honestly (Ra is the stated
example: supreme and solar agree).

**4. ✅ SETTLED — purple is the night of the MIND.** The owner
accepted the defense: wisdom is won in the dark (Odin on the tree,
Thoth's night knowledge, Sorrow riding purple at midnight), while
the REALM of darkness now has its own home at the dual's shadow
face. The Pride-on-Sunday defense also stands (the scale's
self-exaltation pole IS Lucifer, the bible_dark Sun); the
binding-by-binding review remains available on request but no
binding is currently challenged.

**Sunday — Zeus.** The sovereign, full stop. Justice is the king's
virtue, Pride his recorded sin (the myths are a catalog of it),
Glory his weather. No serious alternative.

**Monday — Poseidon.** The owner's own call and the strongest forged

Fear is what the sea becomes, and the Moon literally drags his tides.
Alternative considered: Artemis (Selene-conflation) — rejected: it
repeats the "literal counterpart" mistake the Pantheon exists to fix.

**Tuesday — Artemis (decided by the max-coverage principle).** The
Planetary roster already seats Ares, so the Pantheon takes the
huntress and the pair of rosters covers both — exactly the owner's
rule. The seat argument holds on its own: the hunt is a soldier's
craft (the stalk, the drawn bow, the strike — Tuesday's Zeal), and
her punishments (Actaeon, Niobe) carry the seat's Wrath. The owner's
hunting-is-green reading remains on record as her alternative chair
(Saturday, vs Demeter) — but with Hera now taking Friday, Demeter's
Saturday is the roster's only green anchor, so Tuesday is Artemis's
recommended seat.

**Wednesday — Athena.** Wisdom is the seat's own word — the goddess
of the seat's virtue, strategy as applied intellect, the owl as the
purple night-mind. Cost: Hermes yields (he keeps his Planetary seat).
Alternative: keep Hermes (fame, trade=Merchant estate) — but then
Athena, the second-most-important Olympian, has NO seat, which is the
exact failure the owner named.

**Thursday — Apollo.** Delphi makes him the Priest estate incarnate;
prophecy and music given freely (Generosity), plague-arrows and
hubris-punishment when the yellow overflows (Excess). Alternative:
Dionysos (Excess personified, Joy's god) — colorful but seats the
VICE, not the figure's rank; Apollo outranks him on fame and breadth.

**Friday — Hera (max-coverage, and a perfect fit).** Aphrodite
already holds the Planetary Friday, so the Pantheon seats the queen
of marriage — Love as institution rather than passion — and the
seat's vice writes itself: JEALOUSY is literally Hera's signature
myth, told a hundred ways. The most famous remaining omission,
seated by her own defining trait.

**Saturday — Demeter.** Green, grain, the Patience of the harvest,
Renewal = Persephone's return — the seat's whole bundle in one myth.
(The Artemis-at-Saturday variant stays on record under Tuesday.)

**Dual — Zeus / Hades (crystallized, doctrine point 1).** The throne
above and the throne below: the same royal blood wearing the bright
face and the shadow face, exactly as Helios wears Phaethon and the
cosmos Sun wears the Black Hole. Hades stands at 24h all week and
opaque every Sunday — the owner's two home figurines, Zeus and
Hades, are the dial's own Sunday pair. (Prometheus / Zeus-Xenios,
the earlier dual candidates, retire to the Wider lane.)

**⚖ Ninth — with Hades promoted to the dual, the ninth reopens.**
The crystallized doctrine prefers a UNION ninth where the mythology
offers one. Proposal: **Dionysos the twice-born** — god by father,
mortal by mother, killed and reborn, holding both natures in one
body; the Greek Union figure, and another famous name seated.
Alternatives: keep **Hades** as the ninth in the Planetary mode only
(mode-dependent ninths — advise against, one ninth per theme keeps
the Encyclopedia honest) or **Pan** (the only god the myths declare
DEAD — a strong Excluded form). Owner picks: Dionysos (rec) / Pan /
Hades-stays.
