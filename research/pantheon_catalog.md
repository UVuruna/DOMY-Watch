# The Pantheon Catalog — Both Rosters, Seat by Seat

**Purpose (owner 2026-07-15):** the review document — every theme,
every seat, BOTH modes side by side: **Planetary** (the shipped
roster, seated as day-ruler counterparts) and **Pantheon** (the new
roster, seated by the figure's REAL rank and role in its own
mythology, bound to our seat archetypes). Deliberately more than
needed: alternatives, pros and cons, and every open dilemma flagged
`⚖` — we decide those together. Option names APPROVED: **Pantheon /
Planetary** (SR Panteon / Planetarni), default Planetary.

## Doctrine crystallization (owner round two, 2026-07-15)

**1. The Underworld has a home — the DUAL's shadow face.** The
owner's alarm was right (of the three great Greek brothers we seated
none but Zeus) and the fix was already inside our own system: the
Sunday dual was never strictly Ruler/Servant — that is the
professions/planets theology only. Our shipped dual pairs read
Helios/**Phaethon** (the fallen son), Sól/**Skoll** (the devourer),
Sun/**Black Hole** (the collapse pole), Gold/**Raw Ore**,
Lucifer/**Judas**. The dual is THE BRIGHT FACE AND ITS SHADOW, theme
by theme. Therefore: **the Pantheon Greek dual is Zeus / Hades** —
the throne above and the throne below, same royal blood, two realms.
Hades stands on the dial at 24h all week and OPAQUE every Sunday —
a real, visible seat, not a footnote. (Where a mythology has a true
underworld king, this pattern is now available to it.)

**2. The Ninth, crystallized — the one OUTSIDE the circle, in one of
two forms.** The owner's Sigma reading is adopted: Sigma is NOT a
lesser exile — he is the UNION of Alpha and Omega, both poles in one
body, possibly stronger than Alpha. Half our ninths already read
this way: the Philosopher's Stone (all seven metals completed),
Melchizedek (king AND priest, no lineage), the Big Bang (every plate
in one point), Legion (the dark union — many in one). The others are
the EXCLUDED form (Set, the Cat, Ophiuchus, the Unknown God). So the
doctrine now names both faces: **the Ninth is outside the week's
circle — either as the Excluded One or as the Union of the week's
two poles (the Judas–Lucifer Union figure); the Union is the nobler
form and preferred where the mythology offers one.** Sigma's article
gets reframed accordingly.

**3. The max-coverage principle (owner).** Where the symbolism fits
CLEANLY, the Pantheon roster prefers a figure the Planetary roster
does NOT already seat — the two rosters together should cover as
many first-rank figures as possible. A repeat is allowed only where
no fresh figure carries the seat honestly (Ra is the stated example:
supreme and solar agree).

**4. The archetype table itself is open for review.** The owner
confirmed blue-Fear and orange-Wrath, doubts purple-Greed, and
floated Pride-toward-yellow. One defense worth stating before that
review: PRIDE is the throne's sin by our deepest canon — the scale's
self-exaltation pole IS Lucifer, the bible_dark SUN; moving Pride
off Sunday would unstitch the Judas–Lucifer scale, the bible_dark
mirror and the virtue/sin/mood wheels at once. Purple-Wisdom also
has a defense: purple is the night of the MIND — wisdom won in the
dark (Odin hangs in darkness for the runes; Thoth is the NIGHT
knowledge; Sorrow rides purple at midnight) — while the REALM of
darkness now has its own home at the dual's shadow face (point 1).
If any binding still feels wrong after this round, we run a formal
binding-by-binding review before any roster is finalized.

With the dual and the max-coverage principle, the TWO rosters
together now seat: Zeus, Hera, Poseidon, Hades, Athena, Apollo,
Artemis, Ares, Aphrodite, Hermes, Demeter, Helios, Selene, Cronus,
Phaethon — the owner's "three brothers" complaint is fully closed
and only Hephaestus and Hestia remain for the Wider lane.

**Sunday — Zeus.** The sovereign, full stop. Justice is the king's
virtue, Pride his recorded sin (the myths are a catalog of it),
Glory his weather. No serious alternative.

**Monday — Poseidon.** The owner's own call and the strongest forged
link in the set: the blue seat is the SEA's — Serenity is calm water,
Fear is what the sea becomes, and the Moon literally drags his tides.
Alternative considered: Artemis (Selene-conflation) — rejected: it
repeats the "literal counterpart" mistake the Pantheon exists to fix.

**Tuesday — Artemis (decided by the max-coverage principle).** The
Planetary roster already seats Ares, so the Pantheon takes the
huntress and the pair of rosters covers both — exactly the owner's
rule. The seat argument holds on its own: the hunt is a soldier's
craft (the stalk, the drawn bow, the strike — Tuesday's Zeal), and
her punishments (Actaeon, Niobe) carry the seat's Wrath. The owner's
hunting-is-green reading remains on record as her alternative chair
(Saturday, vs Demeter) — but with Hera now taking Friday, Demeter's
Saturday is the roster's only green anchor, so Tuesday is Artemis's
recommended seat.

**Wednesday — Athena.** Wisdom is the seat's own word — the goddess
of the seat's virtue, strategy as applied intellect, the owl as the
purple night-mind. Cost: Hermes yields (he keeps his Planetary seat).
Alternative: keep Hermes (fame, trade=Merchant estate) — but then
Athena, the second-most-important Olympian, has NO seat, which is the
exact failure the owner named.

**Thursday — Apollo.** Delphi makes him the Priest estate incarnate;
prophecy and music given freely (Generosity), plague-arrows and
hubris-punishment when the yellow overflows (Excess). Alternative:
Dionysos (Excess personified, Joy's god) — colorful but seats the
VICE, not the figure's rank; Apollo outranks him on fame and breadth.

**Friday — Hera (max-coverage, and a perfect fit).** Aphrodite
already holds the Planetary Friday, so the Pantheon seats the queen
of marriage — Love as institution rather than passion — and the
seat's vice writes itself: JEALOUSY is literally Hera's signature
myth, told a hundred ways. The most famous remaining omission,
seated by her own defining trait.

**Saturday — Demeter.** Green, grain, the Patience of the harvest,
Renewal = Persephone's return — the seat's whole bundle in one myth.
(The Artemis-at-Saturday variant stays on record under Tuesday.)

**Dual — Zeus / Hades (crystallized, doctrine point 1).** The throne
above and the throne below: the same royal blood wearing the bright
face and the shadow face, exactly as Helios wears Phaethon and the
cosmos Sun wears the Black Hole. Hades stands at 24h all week and
opaque every Sunday — the owner's two home figurines, Zeus and
Hades, are the dial's own Sunday pair. (Prometheus / Zeus-Xenios,
the earlier dual candidates, retire to the Wider lane.)

**⚖ Ninth — with Hades promoted to the dual, the ninth reopens.**
The crystallized doctrine prefers a UNION ninth where the mythology
offers one. Proposal: **Dionysos the twice-born** — god by father,
mortal by mother, killed and reborn, holding both natures in one
body; the Greek Union figure, and another famous name seated.
Alternatives: keep **Hades** as the ninth in the Planetary mode only
(mode-dependent ninths — advise against, one ninth per theme keeps
the Encyclopedia honest) or **Pan** (the only god the myths declare
DEAD — a strong Excluded form). Owner picks: Dionysos (rec) / Pan /
Hades-stays.

archetype embodied; her magic is Monday's blue calm; arguably the
most venerated Egyptian deity. ⚖ *Placement question, not membership:*
Isis could claim FRIDAY instead (the loving wife as Love's seat),
which would return Khonsu to Monday and keep Hathor out. My
recommendation stands — Isis's defining act is the healing, Hathor's
is the music: Monday-Isis + Friday-Hathor uses both at full strength.

**Tuesday — Horus.** The avenger's war — Courage with a cause, the
falcon the world knows. Montu's only claim was the literal one.

**Wednesday — Thoth ✓.** Wisdom's own scribe, unchallenged.

**Thursday — Anubis (the owner's call, seconded).** "If Amun is in,
Anubis cannot be out" — agreed, and the seat argument holds: the
rite-master who performs the passage IS the Priest estate; the
weighing of the heart is Thursday's Generosity ledger read from the
other side. Amun (the hidden temple god — the priest's PRIEST) moves
to the Wider-Pantheon lane; his fame outside Egyptology is thinner
than Anubis's everywhere else.

**⚖ Max-coverage variants (owner's principle applied to the two
remaining repeats):** Friday — **Bastet** instead of the Hathor
repeat (music, joy, protection, the cat the world knows; Hathor
stays Planetary Friday); Saturday — **Geb** instead of the Osiris
repeat (the green earth itself; but Osiris's grain-death-Renewal is
the stronger seat myth). Recommendation: take **Bastet**, keep
**Osiris** — one repeat is worth the seat's best story. Sun stays
Ra in both (the owner's sanctioned repeat). ⚖ Dual under the
crystallized doctrine: Egypt has a true underworld option too —
**Ra / Osiris** (the sun above / the lord below, whom Ra visits
every night in the Duat) instead of sharing Afu-Ra; but Osiris
already holds Saturday, so the recommendation stays **Afu-Ra
shared** (the night-barque IS Ra's own shadow face).

**Saturday — Osiris ✓.** Grain, death, Renewal — the seat's story.

**Sunday — Odin.** The Allfather; Pride that hanged itself on the
tree for wisdom — both faces in one figure. Dual proposal: **Odin the
Wanderer** — the one-eyed beggar at the door is the Servant face of
the same body (the cleanest dual structure in the whole catalog).

**⚖ Monday — Hel, against Njord and Frigg.**
- *For Hel:* Monday's dark face is FEAR, and she is its throne; the
  half-dead queen keeps the stillest Calm; hugely famous today.
- *For Njord:* the sea god — the same blue-water logic that seats
  Poseidon; but his fame is a shadow of Hel's.
- *For Frigg:* the mother who tried to heal fate itself (the
  mistletoe oaths) — the Physician reading; quieter fame.
- **Recommendation:** Hel — the seat's shadow face is a legitimate
  claim (Ares holds Tuesday the same way) and the Poseidon logic is
  already used once; twice would look like a formula.

**Tuesday — Thor.** The fighter the world knows; Courage, Zeal, and
enough Wrath stories to fill the vice. Tyr's courage (the hand in the
wolf's mouth) is nobler — but it is ONE act, and Thursday now honors
it better.

**Wednesday — Loki.** The cunning purple mind; Greed/trickery is the
seat's vice and his résumé. Odin vacates for the throne.

**Thursday — Tyr.** The oath-keeper who GAVE his hand — Generosity by
sacrifice, the priest of law. Alternative: Heimdall (the watchman,
Gjallarhorn as the temple horn) — thinner claim, Wider-Pantheon lane.

**Saturday — Freyr.** THE harvest god — green Patience, the field's
Renewal; ends the week the way Demeter does for Greece.

**⚖ Friday, max-coverage variant — Frigg instead of the Freya
repeat:** the exact Hera move — Planetary Friday keeps Freya
(passion), Pantheon Friday seats **Frigg** (marriage, the mother who
wove oaths around her son — Love as devotion; her grief is the
seat's shadow). This also removes Frigg from the Monday contest,
leaving Hel's claim there cleaner. Recommendation: **Frigg**.

**Sunday — Perun / Monday — Veles.** The supreme thunderer takes the
throne and the great duality maps onto the dial's own axis: storm
above, water and the under-world below — Veles on the blue seat is
the Slavic Poseidon move, and here it is ALSO the culture's central
myth (the Perun–Veles combat), which makes it firm.

**⚖ Wednesday — Svarog (soft).** The smith as the making MIND —
craft as Wednesday's wisdom. Honest doubt: a forge is not obviously
purple, and Veles (the native trickster-mind) would fit the seat
better but is spent on Monday. Alternative: leave Wednesday to
**Hors** (planetary reuse) and give Svarog the Wider-Pantheon lane —
but then the Slavic Pantheon adds only two new figures and the owner
explicitly wanted Svarog IN. Recommendation: Svarog, acknowledged as
the least-forged link in this catalog.

**Thursday — Dažbog.** *Dažd-bog* — literally "the god who gives":
Thursday's Generosity by NAME. The strongest single-word claim in
the entire document.

**⚖ Friday — Lada vs Mokoš.**
- *For Lada:* the folk-songs' love goddess — Love's seat by name and
  wedding custom. Scholarly caveat: her ancient attestation is
  debated (possibly a folklore back-formation).
- *For Mokoš:* unquestionably ancient and major, but she is fate,
  moisture and women's WORK — Wednesday/Saturday energies more than
  red Friday passion. **Recommendation:** Lada, with the caveat
  stated; if the owner wants only attested deities, keep Mokoš ✓.

**⚖ Dual.** Perun's Servant face has no obvious native twin.
Options: keep the theme's existing Old Dažbog dual serving both
rosters (cheapest, and the winter-sun elder reads as ANY sun's
servant face), or a Perun-specific "Quiet Perun" (the idol asleep
until spring thunder). Recommendation: share Old Dažbog.
