# The Emotion Wheel — Circumplex Notes (for the article writers)

The owner dropped an emotion wheel (`UV/Emotions.png`, his personal
folder, gitignored) and asked what it offers our web. It is a known
scientific instrument: the **Geneva Emotion Wheel** (Scherer, Univ.
of Geneva), built on **Russell's circumplex model of affect**
(1980): every emotion is a point on TWO crossed axes — valence
(Pleasant ↔ Unpleasant) and arousal (Activated ↔ Calm) — circle
size grading intensity outward. Twenty emotion families around the
rim; everything between two poles, leaning toward one — exactly the
owner's reading.

## What it confirms

- **The geometry is ours.** Two crossed axes, opposites across the
  center, in-betweens on the diagonals — the dial's own
  diagonal-opposition canon, drawn by psychology. Articles may say
  so plainly: science draws the same wheel we do.
- **The four quadrants ARE the four temperaments** (Seasons paint):
  Activated-Unpleasant = choleric (anger, hate, contempt);
  Activated-Pleasant = sanguine (interest, amusement, joy, pride);
  Calm-Unpleasant = melancholic (sadness, guilt, regret, shame,
  disappointment); Calm-Pleasant = phlegmatic (contentment, relief,
  compassion, admiration, love-at-rest). Each temperament's article
  can enumerate its quadrant as its EMOTIONAL FAMILY.
- **Contempt** (prezir) sits activated-unpleasant — our One Soul
  shadow of Respect, confirmed in position; **Pride** sits
  pleasant-activated — the bright face our Gordost darkens.

## Idea sparks (unadopted until the owner picks)

- **Intensity ladders**: the wheel grades each emotion small→large;
  articles can name the ladder (interest → excitement; annoyance →
  anger → rage) — pairs well with our glow/opacity language (a lit
  wedge IS an intensified mood).
- The four IN-BETWEEN emotions the owner highlighted (regret,
  admiration, pride, contempt on the quadrant diagonals) mirror the
  octa's diagonal seats — material if the mood wheel ever wants an
  eight-fold reading aligned to the Compass.
