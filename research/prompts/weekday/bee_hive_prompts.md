# Bee Hive — the 14th Weekday Theme (Gemini)

The owner's verdict: the hive is IN, with two of his own calls that
override the first sketch — the **Drone takes Friday/Venus** (one of
the rare males on the whole dial, standing on the day of love: his
entire existence is a single nuptial flight, given whole and paid
with his life) and the **Queen's Sunday dual is the Cleaner**, the
youngest worker. The ethology seals his choice: a bee's FIRST task,
hours after hatching, is to clean the very cell she was born in —
the mother of the whole hive above, the newest daughter scrubbing
her own cradle below.

## Three hooks no other theme has

1. **The career IS the clock (temporal polyethism).** A worker
   changes profession as she ages: cleaner → nurse → builder →
   guard → forager, until she dies in the field with tattered
   wings. Her role is literally her age. The week wears her whole
   life: the Sunday dual is her FIRST day, the Saturday forager her
   LAST — the week bookended by birth and death, while the Queen
   alone lives in years.
2. **Bees build the dial's own geometry.** The comb is hexagons —
   the very shape the hexagram pointer wears. The border ring is
   honeycomb; the theme carries the instrument's signature.
3. **The waggle dance is a solar compass.** The scout's dance
   encodes the food's direction as an ANGLE TO THE SUN — straight
   up the comb means "toward the sun". A living sundial inside the
   dark of the hive, on Mercury's day.

## Style — the Fifth Metal Theme

Weathered **bronze sculptural relief on dark cracked stone** (the
greek/norse/chinese/wolf house material) so the gold/silver hue swap
works unchanged; plus a `colored/` set and the `dual/` pair.

**Border language (collides with nothing):** a bronze ring worked as
a band of **hexagonal honeycomb cells — some open, some wax-capped —
with tiny worker bees crawling the lattice**, and four small roundels
bearing the day's planetary glyph.

**Family resemblance of the duals, diegetic this time:** there is no
sky inside a hive, so the Cleaner's plate carries the eclipse as the
hive would write it — **one dark empty cell rimmed with a thin
gilded ring of fresh wax**, the hive's own eclipsed sun.

**Metal-swap note (technical):** the gold/silver styles hue-swap the
WARM BRONZE pixels (hue window ~10–60°). Keep non-metal accents COOL
or RED (moon-pale wax, ember red — below the window); the gilded
comb-sun on the Queen and the gilded cell-ring on the Cleaner swap
WITH the metal by design, like the Alpha's sun disc.

**Colored arc — out of the amber dark:** the colored set walks the
career from the hive's glowing amber interior to the open blue sky:
Cleaner and Nurse deep inside the honey-lit dark, Builder at the
comb's growing edge, Guard on the threshold, Scout and Drone and
Forager under open sky. The week opens like the hive does.
