# Cosmos Medallion Prompts (Gemini)

COSMOS is a new weekday theme (16th): each day becomes a deep-sky
object mapped to that day's own virtue/mood, the same "one theme, one
object family" pattern as wolf/bee/elephant. **Two looks, two jobs:**
the owner downloads REAL astrophotography himself for the theme's
actual art (not this document's job) — these prompts are the
**artistic medallion** register instead: aged bronze relief on dark
cracked stone, the fourth-metal-theme material wolf/greek/norse/chinese
already share so the gold/silver hue-swap keeps working unchanged,
plus a `colored/` full-color arc.

**Celestial canon (already fixed elsewhere in the project, restated
here so the two deep-sky prompt sheets agree):** the Sun is the zero
of the self-love/self-hate scale — light given away, neither exploding
nor collapsing; the **supernova** is a star destroying itself outward
(`research/bible_dark_prompts.md` names this Lucifer's death, the
self-exaltation pole); the **black hole** is a star collapsing into
itself, the light not allowed to leave (that file names it Judas's
death, sitting at the dial's bottom). COSMOS reuses both images but
maps them by day-virtue rather than by scripture-mirror: the black
hole stays on the **Sunday servant** seat (dial's bottom, same logic),
while the supernova moves to **Tuesday** for its Zeal/Wrath charge —
the two sheets are independent day-maps sharing one astrophysical
vocabulary, not the same roster twice.

## Style — Star-Chart Border & Metal-Swap

Same **weathered bronze sculptural relief on dark cracked stone**
register as wolf/greek/norse/chinese, plus a `colored/` full-color
arc and the cross-theme `dual/` convention (see any theme's own
prompt sheet, e.g. `greek_prompts.md` or `profession_prompts.md`).

**Border language (must not collide with the other bronze themes):**
no paw-prints (wolf), no meander (greek), no huiwen (chinese). The
Cosmos ring is **a fine engraved star-chart — thin constellation
lines linking tiny six-point stars** — with four small roundels
bearing the day's planetary glyph, same roundel convention every
other bronze theme uses.

**Metal-swap note (technical), same rule as wolf:** gold/silver
styles hue-swap the WARM BRONZE pixels (hue window ~10–60°); every
non-metal accent in the prompts below is kept COOL or RED, below that
window. The deliberate exception is the bright ring/beam that carries
each plate's "light" reading — the Sun's streaming rays, the black
hole's accretion ring, the eclipse corona — each called out below to
hue-swap WITH the metal, exactly like the wolf Alpha/Omega sun-and-
corona pair.

**File-stem rule (deviates from wolf on purpose):** wolf/greek/norse
capitalize stems because those themes name proper nouns (gods,
named wolves). Cosmos objects are common nouns, so stems stay
lowercase snake_case — the same convention `bible_theme_prompts.md`
and `bible_dark_prompts.md` already use for their casts.

**Drop (wiring follows once the art lands):**
- `assets/weekday/cosmos/primary/` — `sun.png`, `nebula.png`, `supernova.png`,
  `pulsar.png`, `galaxy.png`, `binary_stars.png`, `comet.png`
- `assets/weekday/cosmos/primary/dual/black_hole.png`
- `assets/weekday/cosmos/colored/` — same seven stems

## Bronze plates

### Sunday, ruler face — the Sun (`sun.png`)

The zero of the scale: light given away steadily, no eruption, no
collapse.

```
Ornate circular medallion, weathered bronze sculptural relief on dark cracked stone, photorealistic render, perfectly centered, isolated on white background. Center: the Sun as a living body filling the plate, its face threaded with slow granulation cells and shallow looping prominences, unhurried rays streaming outward past the rim in every direction like water poured freely from a full vessel — no flare bursting past the disc's edge, no eruption, only the calm ceaseless bestowal of a star that neither explodes nor collapses. Border: bronze ring carved as a fine engraved star-chart — thin constellation lines linking tiny six-point stars — with four small roundels bearing the Sun glyph. Palette: aged bronze dominant; the radiant disc and its streaming rays are the one element meant to hue-swap WITH the metal on the gold/silver plates.
```

### Sunday, servant face (dual) — Black Hole (`dual/black_hole.png`)

The owner's event-horizon brief: a perfect black disc, thin bright
accretion ring, gravitationally lensed halo — the star that fell into
itself.

```
Ornate circular medallion, weathered bronze sculptural relief on dark cracked stone, photorealistic render, perfectly centered, isolated on white background. Center: a perfect black disc at the very center, left as flat unworked stone with no relief carved into it at all, wrapped tight by a thin brilliant accretion ring; above and below the disc a faint lensed halo of light bends clean around the sphere and folds back on itself, the classic event-horizon silhouette (the M87/Interstellar read) — the last light of a star that fell into itself, none of it allowed to leave. Border: bronze ring carved as a fine engraved star-chart — thin constellation lines linking tiny six-point stars — with four small roundels bearing the Sun glyph, mirroring the Ruler plate. Palette: darkened bronze dominant, the black disc left bare stone; the accretion ring is the one element meant to hue-swap WITH the metal, exactly like the Ruler plate's rays.
```
