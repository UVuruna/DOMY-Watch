# Ancient Religions — the Seven Ancient Faiths Weekday Theme (silver-and-gold plates)

**Rename (this rework):** this theme was previously **Mysteries**
(`WEEKDAY_THEME_TITLES["religion_alt"] = "Mysteries"`); the owner's
Religion rework (GO 2026-07-15, `research/pantheon_catalog.md` → "The
Religion rework") renames it **Ancient Religions**, superseding
"Mysteries" — the code round that follows this sheet updates the title
constant to `"Ancient Religions"`. The rename reflects the roster more
than it changes it: six of the seven traditions were already ancient
faiths (Mithraism, Druidism, Zoroastrianism, Shamanism, Babylon,
Voodoo); Sikhism — a living faith, ~26 M strong today — is the one seat
that never quite fit "ancient," and this rework promotes it OUT to
Creeds' freed Friday seat. Its replacement, the **Eleusinian
Mysteries**, is the genuine article: the greatest mystery cult of
antiquity, sealed for two thousand years and never once written down.
The Ninth also changes: **The Unknown God** relocates IN from Creeds
(the Athenian altar is literally ancient — Acts 17) and the theme's old
Ninth, **The Lost Mystery**, is **SUPERSEDED** — it folds into the
Unknown God's article (the owner's own framing: "they are the same
thought told twice") and its plate stays on disk as historical art. This
sheet is the ONE complete generation-and-reuse manifest for the
reworked roster: all seven roster prompts below, the unchanged Dual,
and the new Ninth. Canon source remains `Database/symbolism.json`
(`articles.religion_alt.<body>.base` / `.faces`) for the six unmoved
plates; **jupiter (Eleusinian Mysteries) has no canon paragraph yet** —
this sheet's prompt is written straight from the owner's rework brief
(wheat sheaf, kykeon cup, torchlit procession, Demeter-and-Kore
silhouettes) pending a later symbolism.json round.

**Border identity:** a darker, starlit-stone mystery-cult register —
mostly gold-and-silver relief against night-dark or starred lapis
fields, the torch-and-serpent mood several of these seven traditions
actually share (Mithraism's serpent lunging at the bull,
Zoroastrianism's altar flame, Shamanism's firelit tent, Eleusis' own
torchlit procession). As before, **each tradition keeps its OWN
canonical emblem and border** rather than one borrowed ring —
Mithraism's raven/hound/scorpion/star tauroctony rim, Druidism's Celtic
knotwork threaded with fly-agaric, Zoroastrianism's flame-engraved rim,
Shamanism's feather-and-antler rim, Eleusis' torch-and-poppy rim,
Babylon's lion-and-palm rim, Voodoo's cowrie rim. Shamanism (firelit
ember totem) and Voodoo (copper-and-burlap doll) are the two the canon
itself pulls off the gold-and-silver register — kept as written, not
smoothed over.

**Style note:** single-look silver-and-gold family — no gold/silver
hue-swap, no `colored/` companion set. (Sikhism's plate, which used to
carry this same register here, has moved out to Creeds — see
`religion_prompts.md` — where it is now the one flagged exception to
THAT theme's silver-only rule.)

**Respect non-negotiables (every plate):** no legible text, script, or
lettering of any kind — the canon's cuneiform-star and rune mentions
become abstract, non-alphabetic surface texture instead of readable
type. No caricature of any tradition, living or historic; Voodoo in
particular is rendered with the dignity the article insists on ("the
owner's medallion restores dignity to the emblem outsiders know
best"), never as a mockery prop. The Unknown God's altar (below) keeps
its face completely blank/worn — **strictly no lettering**, since the
whole point of the seat is a god no cult has ever named.

Files land in `assets/weekday/religion/secondary/` — the `religion_alt`
theme's OWN directory (`WEEKDAY_THEME_DIRS["religion_alt"] =
"religion/secondary"`), a sibling of Creeds' `religion/primary/`, not
the same folder. This rework moves two files, and both moves are
**implementation work — not art regeneration**:

- **sikhism.png** — leaves this tree entirely; see `religion_prompts.md`
  for the full move note (`religion/secondary/sikhism.png` →
  `religion/primary/sikhism.png`).
- **unknown_god.png** — arrives here as a real cross-tree move,
  `religion/primary/unknown_god.png` → `religion/secondary/unknown_god.png`,
  becoming this theme's Ninth plate; its Creeds-era prompt border text
  (which lists Creeds' six/seven faith emblems — chain of tools,
  strapwork, lotus, bagua, Om, cross, star) is kept byte-identical per
  the reseat instruction even though those emblems belong to the OTHER
  theme — flagged below and in the closing report, not silently fixed.
