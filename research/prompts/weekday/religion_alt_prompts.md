# Mysteries — the Seven Alternate Faiths Weekday Theme (silver-and-gold plates)

The Mysteries set — Mithraism, Druidism, Zoroastrianism, Shamanism,
Sikhism, Babylon, Voodoo, plus the Sunday dual Corax — predates the
prompt-sheet convention entirely; this sheet regenerates all eight
plates from the canon in `Database/symbolism.json`
(`articles.religion_alt.<body>.base` / `.faces`) so the owner can drop
fresh renders straight over the existing files. Menu title (owner
rename 2026-07-13): `WEEKDAY_THEME_TITLES["religion_alt"] = "Mysteries"`.

**Border identity:** a darker, starlit-stone mystery-cult register —
mostly gold-and-silver relief against night-dark or starred lapis
fields, the torch-and-serpent mood several of these seven traditions
actually share (Mithraism's serpent lunging at the bull, Zoroastrianism's
altar flame, Shamanism's firelit tent). As with Creeds, **each
tradition keeps its OWN canonical emblem and border** rather than one
borrowed ring — Mithraism's raven/hound/scorpion/star tauroctony rim,
Druidism's Celtic knotwork threaded with fly-agaric, Zoroastrianism's
flame-engraved rim, Shamanism's feather-and-antler rim, Sikhism's
lotus-and-khanda rim, Babylon's lion-and-palm rim, Voodoo's cowrie rim.
Shamanism (firelit ember totem) and Voodoo (copper-and-burlap doll) are
the two the canon itself pulls off the gold-and-silver register — kept
as written, not smoothed over.

**Style note:** single-look silver-and-gold family — no gold/silver
hue-swap, no `colored/` companion set.

**Respect non-negotiables (every plate):** no legible text, script, or
lettering of any kind — the canon's cuneiform-star and rune mentions
become abstract, non-alphabetic surface texture instead of readable
type. No caricature of any tradition, living or historic; Voodoo in
particular is rendered with the dignity the article insists on ("the
owner's medallion restores dignity to the emblem outsiders know
best"), never as a mockery prop.

Files land in the SAME shared `assets/weekday/religion/` folder as
Creeds (per `WEEKDAY_THEME_DIRS`, both themes point at one directory).
Stems below are exact and lowercase, matching
`WEEKDAY_THEME_FILES["religion_alt"]`.

## The Ninth — The Lost Mystery (`lost_mystery.png`)

Owner 8+1 extension (2026-07-14): the Lost Mystery is the Ninth of
the Mysteries set, kept in the same single-look silver-and-gold
register as the other seven traditions — this theme carries no
colored arc either. Use undecided: legend third plate / Compass
ninth seat / Prism triptych. It is the Eleusinian secret that was
never written down, the one mystery that kept itself even from
history; no duality or trinity from this dial's Judas–Lucifer axis
has a natural claim on a secret that by definition left no other pole
to mirror it against.

```
Ornate circular medallion, gold-and-silver relief on night-dark stone, photorealistic render, perfectly centered, isolated on white background, square 1:1 aspect ratio. Center: a sealed stone chest resting on a low altar inside a torch-lit hall, its lid bound shut with an unbroken cord and a wax seal left uncarved and blank, a single sheaf of barley laid across the lid, the hall's far end fading into darkness where the initiates would have stood — nothing shown of what was kept. Border: a plain rim of interlaced barley ears and closed poppy pods, no lettering, no figure, no emblem borrowed from any of the other six mysteries. Palette: gold-and-silver relief on night-dark stone dominant; the wax seal the only pale, unmarked accent. No text, no watermark.
```
