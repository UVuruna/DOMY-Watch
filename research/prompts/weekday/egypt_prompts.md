# Egyptian Gods — the Theban Weekday Theme (bronze plates + colored)

The Egyptian set — Ra, Khonsu, Montu, Thoth, Amun, Hathor, Osiris,
plus the Sunday dual Afu-Ra — predates the prompt-sheet convention
entirely and, unlike Greek/Norse, never got a `colored/` companion set;
this sheet regenerates the eight bronze plates from the canon in
`Database/symbolism.json` so the owner can drop fresh renders straight
over the existing files, and adds the eight colored plates the theme
was missing.

**Border identity:** a bronze ring carved as a continuous
**hieroglyph cartouche band**, running clockwise, broken by four
roundels alternating an **ankh** and a **scarab** in relief, each
cradling the day's planetary glyph — distinct from every other bronze
theme's border (no meander, no knotwork, no paw-prints).

**Style note:** aged bronze relief is the base register because this
is the metal-swap family — gold and silver variants hue-swap the same
warm bronze pixels programmatically, so no separate gold/silver
prompts are needed here.

sharing the same bronze and colored looks as the other eight, not an
encyclopedia-only figure. Use undecided: legend third plate / Compass
ninth seat / Prism triptych. He is the excluded murderer brother —
Osiris already sits Saturday's throne, mummiform and at peace — the
Cain of this set, envy turned to violence rather than the other way
round; read as a triad rather than a single pole, Set's violent
self-assertion and Osiris's total passivity in death echo the
Judas–Lucifer axis from opposite Egyptian ends, with Horus, the
avenging son who is never on the dial either, standing as their
unshown Union.
