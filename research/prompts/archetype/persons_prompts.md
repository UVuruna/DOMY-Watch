# Prism Archetype Prompts (Gemini) — The Persons, the six on the paint hexa

The PRISM PAINT pointer's archetype set (owner canon 2026-07-16, canon
in [The DOMY Canon](../../../CANON.md)): where the Trinity pointer
carries three persons of a courtroom, the Prism paint hexagram carries
SIX — the same wheel-web spoken as love and its ruin. Paint carries
the WORLD's order; the six seats are laid on the hexa PAINT hues, each
a person and a quality (the two-row canon: the person, then the
quality — Michael / Courage). Owner's seating, verbatim intent:

- **The One (God)** — yellow, 12h — **Love**: the shared summit of
  Weakness and Pride, love of others AND love of self at the exact
  point where they become sin; the union of the two loves below.
- **Michael the Archangel** — orange, 16h — **Courage**: the warrior
  of Revelation 12:7, who wars against the dragon.
- **Lucifer** — red, 20h — **Pride**: love turned toward himself.
  His Scale glass window is already ember-red — **REUSE** (see below).
- **the Devil** — purple, 24h — **Hatred**: the shared ROOT of the
  virtue pair; turned inward it drags Humility down, turned outward it
  turns Courage into wrath and overreach.
- **Judas** — blue, 04h — **Weakness (Fear)**: love turned to
  despair. His Scale glass window is already midnight-blue — **REUSE**
  (see below).
- **Jesus** — green, 08h — **Humility**: the one who descended;
  renunciation, the same nature as his Trinity seat.

The diagonals carry the oppositions, and with the persons seated
every diagonal is a PERSON-PAIR (canon anchors, never inscribed in
the art): **The One against the Devil** — Love against Hatred
(12h–24h); **Jesus against Lucifer** — Humility against Pride
(08h–20h), the one who descended against the one who wanted to
ascend: Luke 14:11 *"he that humbleth himself shall be exalted"*
against Isaiah 14:13 *"I will ascend into heaven"*; **Michael against
Judas** — Courage against Fear (04h–16h), the one who fights against
the one who despaired. Revelation 12 holds the whole cast in one
chapter — Michael wars the dragon (12:7) and the Accuser is thrown
down (12:10) — balancing the wheel three-and-three: The One, Jesus
and Michael against Lucifer, the Devil and Judas.

**Color fidelity across registers** (canon, owner 2026-07-16): a
figure that already wears one color KEEPS it where it can, and when a
second register must move it, the article argues the NEW seat from a
DIFFERENT true facet of the same figure. Three of the six seats
invoke this rule and the italic notes below name the facet used.

**Register:** the stained-glass night-window family — the same
register as the Scale glass and the Trinity lancets (see
[Scale Prompts](../badge/scale_badge_prompts.md),
[Trinity Prompts](trinity_prompts.md)); each of the six persons is a
LANCET window in its arm's color (yellow 12h / orange 16h / purple
24h / green 08h — the two REUSED poles are red 20h and blue 04h). A
metal-cameo register can be added later if the owner wants a second
look.

**Drop paths:** `assets/archetype/<source>/persons/` —
`One_Love.png`, `Michael_Courage.png`, `Devil_Hatred.png`,
`Jesus_Humility.png` + the center `Seal.png` (its section below). The
two remaining seats REUSE the Scale glass at
`assets/badge/<source>/scale/glass/` — see the reuse notes.

## The four new persons

*(Color-fidelity note: The One also holds the yellow/gold summit in
the Trinity paint set — but there he is argued as the VEILED JUDGE
who weighs. Here the SAME color is argued from a different true facet:
not judgment but LOVE at its summit — the union of both loves, love of
others and love of self held at the razor's edge where they would
become sin. Same seat, the other face of the one who holds both
natures.)*

```

Radiant yellow, warm amber and white glass throughout, no cold tones. Center: a tall enthroned figure whose face is VEILED in pure golden radiance — never shown — seated high, both arms open in a wide embrace that GATHERS in: one hand drawn to his own breast, the other reaching outward to the world, the two gestures perfectly balanced; at the exact center of his chest a single heart of PERFECTLY CLEAR uncolored glass, plain white daylight passing through it, the brightest point of the window — the place where love of self and love of others meet without tipping into sin. His drapery falls in two warm tones joined seamlessly down the center, gold on one half, honey-amber on the other. Upper left panel: two hands clasped in equal grip; upper right panel: a single lit lamp shared between two shadowed figures. Border: gothic leadwork in dark gold; three rim roundels — a clear heart at the apex, a sun at noon at one side, two joined rings at the other. Palette: radiant yellow, warm amber, white light, dark-gold lead, the clear heart the only uncolored glass. NO lettering anywhere.
```
