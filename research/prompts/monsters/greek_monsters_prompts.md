# Greek Monsters — the Bestiary Weekday Theme (Gemini/ChatGPT)

Owner-approved 2026-07-21 night: a NEW weekday theme, single roster (no
Planetary/Pantheon split — the six weekday seats, a Sunday dual, a
Ninth and a title plate, the same "closed set" shape wolf/bee/elephant/
cosmos already ship). The cast sits by NATURE against the seat's own
color/virtue/vice bundle (CANON.md "The Web of Correspondences"), never
by a literal planetary match — Medusa is not "the moon," she is
Monday's FEAR made flesh.

**Register — a darker sibling of the Olympian family:** the god
medallions (`weekday/greek_prompts.md`) wear aged-bronze relief and
Mediterranean enamel; this bestiary wears the house **night-window**
stained-glass register instead (the same family `era_prompts.md` and
`months_prompts.md` use) — dark jewel-toned glass, the circular window
shape IS the frame — while KEEPING the Olympian family's own **Greek
key (meander) border**, recut as dark leadwork instead of bronze relief
or polished gold. One glance says "Greek"; the dark glass says
"monster," not "god." Every plate carries a `primary/` night-window
version and a `colored/` full-vivid-enamel version, same two-register
convention every other weekday theme ships (owner: "night-window +
colored variants per the theme-family conventions").

**Derivation check (Rule #19):** every plate below is an irreducible
scene — a different figure, a different myth-beat, no two plates share
a composition a formula could derive one from the other. The
`primary`/`colored` split is NOT a tint pass (unlike gold/silver
metal-swap themes): colored is a fresh full-color repaint of the same
scene, the same non-derivable convention every sibling sheet already
uses (`greek_prompts.md`, `cosmos_prompts.md`). Nothing here is a
candidate for algorithmic collapse.

**House rules:** photorealistic render, isolated background (no
transparency-checkerboard artifact), the circular window/badge shape IS
the frame, NO lettering anywhere in any image (the title plate is the
one documented exception in this whole project — see
[Theme Title Prompts](../titles/theme_title_prompts.md) — and even it
lives in a SEPARATE file/root, not here).

**Drop paths:** `assets/weekday/monsters/primary/<Stem>.png` (night-
window) and `assets/weekday/monsters/colored/<Stem>.png` (full enamel)
— both flat, no `dual/` subfolder (DUAL FLATTEN convention, same as
every restructured theme). The title plate is NOT part of this root —
see the cross-reference at the bottom.

The dual is a blood pair, not an allegory: Nemean Lion and Cerberus are
literally BROTHERS, both children of Typhon and Echidna — one guards
the sun's own labor (Heracles' first), the other guards the midnight
door. The Ninth, Pegasus, is the **Excluded** kind (CANON.md "The
Ninth"): born from Medusa's death, he belongs to the myth-web but sits
in neither the monster line nor the god line — "neither monster nor
god, belonging to no world."

## Monday — Medusa (`assets/weekday/monsters/primary/Medusa.png`)

*Fear that petrifies — but her blood is Asclepius's own double
pharmacy, venom from one side, cure from the other; the gaze is the
plate's own center, exactly the myth's own warning.*

```
ROUND rose-window stained-glass medallion, night-window register, photorealistic render, isolated background, the circular window shape IS the frame. Deep indigo-black glass throughout, cold and airless. Center: Medusa's face filling the disc, a crown of living serpents coiling out where hair should be, her eyes open and locked dead-center on the viewer — the gaze itself the whole danger — skin rendered in a faint stone-grey pallor at the edges as if the glass itself is half-petrified. At her throat, two thin glass vials catch what little light survives: the left vial holds a venom-black bead, the right a warm healing amber-gold bead — the same blood, two fates. Border: leadwork rim carved as a continuous Greek key (meander) band, broken by four small roundels bearing the Moon crescent glyph in dark leadwork relief. Palette: indigo-black glass, cold stone-grey, one venom-black bead, one amber-gold bead. NO lettering anywhere.
```
