# Chinese Mythology — the Immortals Weekday Theme (Gemini/ChatGPT)

Owner-approved 2026-07-21 night: a NEW weekday theme, single roster (no
Planetary/Pantheon split — six weekday seats, a Sunday dual, a Ninth and
a title plate, the same "closed set" shape wolf/bee/elephant/cosmos
already ship). NOT to be confused with the existing `chinese`
ZODIAC-ANIMAL theme (`zodiac/chinese_zodiac_prompts.md`, twelve animals,
year-reading) — this is a separate cast of gods, immortals and one
stone-born rebel, seated by NATURE against the seat's own color/virtue/
vice bundle (CANON.md), not by animal-year at all.

**Register — its OWN family, not the Greek key:** cloud-scroll borders
(xiangyun, the auspicious-cloud motif that runs through Chinese temple
and lacquerware ornament) in a jade-and-lacquer palette — deliberately
NOT the Olympian meander, NOT the wolf paw-trail, NOT any bronze theme's
ring, exactly the owner's own instruction. Like the greek monsters
sheet, this theme wears the house **night-window** stained-glass
register (`era_prompts.md`, `months_prompts.md`'s family) rather than a
metal relief, so its dark jewel-glass reads as myth rather than
portraiture. Every plate carries a `primary/` night-window version and
a `colored/` full-vivid version, the same two-register convention every
other weekday theme ships.

**Derivation check (Rule #19):** every plate is an irreducible scene —
a different figure, a different myth-beat; the `primary`/`colored`
split is a fresh full-color repaint, not a tint pass (the same
non-derivable convention `greek_prompts.md`/`cosmos_prompts.md` already
use). Nothing here is a candidate for algorithmic collapse.

**House rules:** photorealistic render, isolated background (no
transparency-checkerboard artifact), the circular window/badge shape IS
the frame, NO lettering anywhere in any image (the title plate is the
one documented exception in this whole project, and it lives in its OWN
file/root — see the cross-reference at the bottom).

**Drop paths:** `assets/weekday/chinese_myth/primary/<Stem>.png`
(night-window) and `assets/weekday/chinese_myth/colored/<Stem>.png`
(full enamel) — both flat, no `dual/` subfolder.

The dual reads exactly like the Bible themes' own Sunday pair
(Ancient of Days / the Son as Servant): not good-against-evil but
authority above meeting authority below — the enlightened crown that
once stopped the Ninth's own rebellion, and the underworld judge who
still keeps his book. The Ninth, Sun Wukong, is the **Excluded** kind
(CANON.md "The Ninth"): "neither god, demon nor man" — he belongs to
every scene in this roster's own myth-cycle (the Peach Banquet he
wrecks, the Buddha's palm that catches him, the underworld ledger
Yanluo keeps on him) yet sits in none of the eight seats.

## Cross-reference — the title plate

**The Peach Banquet** is this theme's TITLE PLATE — the whole-theme
summary image every weekday theme carries a slot for
(`app.encyclopedia._weekday_topic`'s `title_entry`). Chosen over "the
celestial court panorama" (the sheet's own call, per the owner's
brief) for two reasons: (1) a banquet stages as ONE coherent circular
scene the way an abstract "court" cannot — a table, guests, a single
readable composition; (2) it is the roster's own hinge myth — the
Peach Banquet is the event Sun Wukong crashes, eating the peaches of
immortality after being denied a seat at the gods' own table, so the
title plate doubles as the ORIGIN of the Ninth's grievance ("his WISH —
to be acknowledged — is the Ninth's own sin"), the same way the Greek
sheet's Typhon & Echidna plate hooks its dual and Ninth back to their
shared parents. Its brief and drop path live in
[Theme Title Prompts](../titles/theme_title_prompts.md), NOT here — see
that sheet's own entry for `chinese_myth`. Do not duplicate the brief
in this file.
