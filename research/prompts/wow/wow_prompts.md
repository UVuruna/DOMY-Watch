# World of Warcraft — the Three-Faction Weekday Theme (Gemini/ChatGPT)

Owner-sealed rosters, 2026-07-22 (GAMING + CORPORATION SHEET WAVE): a
NEW weekday theme with THREE parallel casts riding the same nine seats
— generalizing `CANON.md`'s existing "Two Rosters" doctrine (Planetary/
Pantheon, picked per the theme's own dropdown slot "like the metals")
from two to three. **Alliance**, **Horde** and **Evil** are three
alternate faces of the SAME nine-seat structure (six weekdays, a
Throne/Mirror dual, an Unfound Ninth) — every seat's color/virtue/vice
bundle stays fixed across all three blocks (Monday is always blue
Fear/Serenity, whichever faction's figure sits there); only the FACE
changes. `Throne` / `Mirror` / `Unfound` are this round's own names for
the doctrine's `Ruler` / `Servant(dual)` / `Ninth(Excluded)` seats —
same structure, vocabulary fit to the theme (a Mirror is a literal
doppelganger for two of the three blocks' own figures, Genn's wolf-self
and Garrosh/Illidan's dark reflections, so "Mirror" reads truer than
"Servant" here).

**Register — TWO deliberately different objects, the project-wide
law (corrected 2026-07-22):** `primary` is a MONOCHROME AGED-BRONZE
CARVED RELIEF — a sculpture in ONE metal (warm bronze patina, darker
recesses, polished highlights; no other colors, no enamel, no
faction paint), every emblem carved as relief in the same bronze —
this is the recolor master the metal-shade pipeline strikes
gold/silver from, the same law `greek_prompts.md`'s planetary bronze
plates set. `colored` is the vivid full-color paint version — a fresh
full-color repaint, never a recolor of the bronze art. The two MUST
read as different objects — a sculpture, not a painting — never
near-duplicates of each other. Each of the THREE blocks keeps its OWN
border FRAME MOTIF — the shape carved into the bronze, never a
separate color family — so the rim's carved SHAPE alone identifies the
block before the center art is even read:

| Block | Border motif (carved in bronze on `primary`; recut in its own metal/paint on `colored`) |
|---|---|
| Alliance | heraldic rampant-lion frame, lions alternating with fleur-quatrefoils |
| Horde | wolf-fang totem frame, wolf-head totems alternating with fang motifs |
| Evil | saronite-and-ice fang frame, jagged shards alternating with cracked ice |

Every plate carries a `primary/` aged-bronze-relief version and a
`colored/` full-vivid-paint version — the same two-register convention
every other weekday theme ships. Each border also carries the day's
own ruling-planet glyph in four small roundels, same house convention
as every other weekday sheet — the per-figure IDENTITY survives
entirely through the carved motif and the scene itself, never through
paint.

**Derivation check (Rule #19):** every plate is an irreducible scene —
a different figure (or, for the rotation seats below, a genuinely
different FACTION), a different myth-beat; the `primary`/`colored`
split is a fresh full-color repaint, not a tint pass, the same
non-derivable convention every sibling weekday sheet uses. The THREE
blocks are not a recolor of one master either — Anduin and Baine and
Kel'Thuzad are three different figures in three different materials,
not one figure repainted three times. Nothing here is a candidate for
algorithmic collapse.

**House rules:** photorealistic render, isolated background (no
transparency-checkerboard artifact), the circular medallion shape IS
the frame, NO lettering anywhere in any image (title plates are the
one documented exception, tracked in their own file — see the
cross-reference at the bottom).

**Drop paths:** `assets/weekday/wow/<block>/primary/<Stem>.png`
(carved relief) and `assets/weekday/wow/<block>/colored/<Stem>.png`
(full paint) — `<block>` is `alliance`, `horde` or `evil`, both flat,
no `dual/` subfolder (DUAL FLATTEN convention, same as every
restructured theme).
