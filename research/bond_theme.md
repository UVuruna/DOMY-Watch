# The Bond — the Pillars of a Man and a Woman (theme definition draft)

The owner's notes (prompt.txt, 2026-07-16 — a conversation he
decided to define and CONNECT to the whole system): *"the key parts
of the relationship between a man and a woman"* — count flexible
(6/7/8/9), his shortlist locked at **Trust, Respect, Support,
Passion**, gratitude named *"the highest and purest form of love"*.
This draft seats the theme on the DOMY wheel — his verdicts
honored, everything else marked proposed.

## The Conjugation Law

The owner's own discovery, generalized into the theme's engine:

> *"Iskrenost je osobina jedinke, a poverenje je njen pandan u
> odnosu."* — honesty is a trait of the individual; trust is its
> counterpart in the relationship.

**Every pillar of the bond is a seat's VIRTUE conjugated between two
persons** — and every pillar's shadow is that seat's VICE conjugated
the same way. The theme does not need new symbolism: the existing
wheel, spoken in the dual. Even the cross-cure axes conjugate (see
below) — the dial's geometry becomes the anatomy of a marriage.

**The center's dual faces (proposed):** the Union shows two faces
like every Sunday — **Odanost (the day face — the union KEPT: the
word held, visible to the world)** and **Bliskost (the night face —
the union FELT: closeness with no defenses up; pending the owner's
Dracula telling)**.

**The cross-cures conjugate** (the existing axes, spoken in the
dual): Trust disarms the Fight and Support cures Suspicion
(Moon↔Mars); Gratitude cures Score-keeping and Tolerance cures
Taking-for-granted (Jupiter↔Mercury); Passion burns away Contempt
and Respect calms Jealousy (Venus↔Saturn); the center heals itself
crosswise, as always.

## The Ninth (owner picks the kind)

- **Union-kind: the Child** — the union of the two made flesh;
  stands outside the eight and is never less important (Ecclesiastes
  4:12: *"a threefold cord is not quickly broken"*).
- **Excluded-kind: Samoća (Loneliness)** — the one outside the
  circle; what every pillar exists against, and what gives the
  circle its meaning.
