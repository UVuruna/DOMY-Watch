# Virtue, Sin and Mood Prompts (Gemini)

Prompts for three new abstract-emblem sets drawn straight from the
cross-cure canon in `SYMBOLISM.md`: the eight **Virtues**, the eight
**Sins** (the arm vices), and the eight **Moods** (the color-mood
table). These are allegorical logos, not portraits of gods or
professions — scales for Justice, a hammer's spark for Zeal, a
darkened sun for Eclipse. Paste one prompt at a time; generate a whole
family (all 8 Virtues, or all 8 Sins, or all 8 Moods) in one chat
session so the set stays consistent.

**Sunday is double.** The center carries the Ruler (bright face) and
the Servant (dark face) as two separate entries in every list — so
each category holds 8 items over 7 days, not 7.

## Design Notes — Three Families, One Cross-Cure Canon

All 24 badges keep the house circular-badge shape from
[Colored Badge Prompts](colored_badge_prompts.md), and every item's
accent color is its day's arm color from the tables above. What sets
the three CATEGORIES apart at a glance — same device the astrology set
uses to lock an element's palette, and the zodiac sets use to lock a
border ring — is a constant **field texture + border metal**, held
fixed across all 8 items in a family:

| Family | Field | Border metal & motif | Reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| Virtue | engraved sunburst — fine rays radiating outward, tinted the day's color | polished **gold** ring, laurel-leaf wreath | ascending, radiant, outward-giving |
| Sin | fissured obsidian — jagged cracks glowing the day's color like magma beneath black stone | blackened **iron** ring, thorned-bramble motif | descending, coiled, shadow leaking through |
| Mood | gradient sky-disc — the hour's own light washing from rim to center | polished **silver** ring, sundial hour-tick motif | atmospheric, temporal, the hour made visible |

Gold reads as virtue's light because it is already the Sun's metal on
this dial (`SYMBOLISM.md` — "gold to the Sun"); silver for mood
because moods are the Moon's domain, the hour's changing light rather
than a fixed moral stance; blackened iron for sin because it is the
one family with no dial metal of its own — corrosion, not alchemy.
Within a family the border stays constant so the three sets read as
one system in the Encyclopedia or Settings grid; the day's arm color
carries all the individual identity, exactly as it already does across
the Colored Badge sets.
