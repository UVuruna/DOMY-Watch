# Wolf Pack — the 13th Weekday Theme (Gemini)

The owner's verdict: the Wolf Pack theme is IN, with **Omega as the
Sunday dual**. The clincher no other theme has: the dial's ring
letters already spell this duality — **M at noon, Ω at midnight** —
so the wolf theme is the first whose Sunday duality stands written
on the clock face itself. Alpha rules the noon seat; the Omega wolf,
the pack's servant in classical ethology, takes the midnight seat
the ring already reserved for him.

**Sigma (no plate):** a wolf equal to the Alpha but OUTSIDE the
hierarchy — "a ruler without a throne". It has no canonical
ethology (it is internet taxonomy), so it gets no art; when the
theme is wired into `Database/symbolism.json`, the Alpha article
takes a paragraph naming Sigma as the modern myth it is.

## Style — the Fourth Metal Theme

Weathered **bronze sculptural relief on dark cracked stone**, the
greek/norse/chinese house material, so the gold/silver hue swap
works unchanged; plus a `colored/` full-color set and the `dual/`
pair, exactly like greek and norse.

**Border language (must NOT collide with the other bronze themes):**
no Celtic knotwork (norse), no meander (greek), no huiwen (chinese).
The wolf ring is **a circling trail of wolf paw-prints running
clockwise, interlaced with small pine sprigs**, with four small
roundels bearing the day's planetary glyph. One glance separates it
from every other theme.

**Metal-swap note (technical):** the gold/silver styles hue-swap the
WARM BRONZE pixels (hue window ~10–60°). Keep every non-metal accent
COOL or RED (moonlight blue, ember red — below the window). The one
exception is deliberate: the gilded sun disc on the Alpha and the
eclipse corona on the Omega swap WITH the metal, exactly as Skoll's
eclipse ring already behaves.

**Family resemblance of the duals:** the Omega plate carries the
thin eclipsed-sun corona ring in its sky — the same mark Skoll bites
and the ore plate hides as a vein.
